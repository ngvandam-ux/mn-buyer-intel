Minnesota Department of Veterans Affairs Agency Profile https://mn.gov/mdva/ AT A GLANCE • The Minnesota Department of Veterans Affairs serves more than 285,000 Veterans and their families. This number represents about 6% of Minnesota’s population • Employs approximately 1,620 staff statewide • The Agency operates eight State Veterans Homes and four State Veterans Cemeteries • To meet the needs of Minnesota’s Veterans, MDVA operates seven major program areas in two divisions. Each program area encompasses many additional Veteran services: - Skilled Nursing and Domiciliary care - Adult Day Center and Resources for Caregivers - Veteran Suicide Prevention and Awareness as well as a Community Health Navigator Program - Veterans Education and Occupational Resources - Federal Veterans Affairs (VA) Claims and Outreach Services - Homeless Veteran Prevention and Assistance Services - State Soldiers Assistance Program PURPOSE The Minnesota Department of Veterans Affairs (MDVA) mission is to “Serve Minnesota Veterans, their dependents and survivors by connecting them with the Federal and State care as well as the benefits they have earned.” Established in 1943, MDVA is a post-wartime agency created to consolidate Veteran programs and services for service members. Since 2007, MDVA has also operated the Minnesota State Veterans Homes. Approximately 41,000 Minnesotans have served since the terrorist attacks on 9/11/01. Veterans from all eras face common as well as unique employment, education, reintegration to society, and health challenges. More than 52% of Minnesota's Veterans are over the age of 65, creating specific, long-term care needs. Veterans from the current conflicts faced multiple deployment, burn pits, and an environment very different from Minnesota. As a result of these conflicts, Veterans may experience short as well as long-term health in addition to mental health challenges, unemployment or underemployment, and complications navigating the Federal Veterans Affairs programs. MDVA’s mission is to also care for military/Veteran families. Many of these families faced both mental fatigue and physical injuries associated with multiple deployments of their Veteran loved one. MDVA’s leaders constantly seek opportunities to pioneer programming to meet the unique needs for Minnesota’s Veterans and their families. STRATEGIES MDVA takes pride in offering innovative solutions specifically tailored to enhance the lives of Minnesota’s Veterans and their families. Many of these innovative solutions can be seen in our 2023 Annual Report (https://mn.gov/mdva/assets/2023-mdva-annual-report_tcm1066-608988.pdf). Through strategic planning modalities, leaders develop as well as implement programming to meet current, ongoing, and future needs. MDVA does this through two divisions: Programs & Services (including Minnesota’s four State Veterans Cemeteries) and Veterans Healthcare (eight Minnesota State Veterans Homes and support programs).State of Minnesota 2 2026-27 Biennial Budget January 2025 Many Veterans are not aware of the benefits they earned through their military service. The Programs & Services Division collaborates with its partners in the Veterans services community to assist Veterans in accessing these benefits. Examples include securing or assisting in the provision of financial, educational, and medical benefits. Additionally, there are an estimated 200 Veterans without safe, stable or affordable housing on any given night in Minnesota. Through partnerships as well as collaboration with stakeholders and the Homeless Veteran Registry, (https:// mn.gov/mdva/resources/homelessnessandprevention/homelessveteranregistry.jsp) MDVA strives to identify along with develop housing plans for Veterans who are homeless or at risk of homelessness. To date, Minnesota has achieved the effective end to Veteran homelessness in eight of 10 continuums of care. MDVA operates four State Veterans Cemeteries (https://mn.gov/mdva/memorials/) located in Duluth, Little Falls, Preston and Redwood Falls. Finally, the Programs & Services Division and partners provide resources to assist Veterans transitioning back to Minnesota, attending higher education, pursuing occupational resources strategies, accessing financial as well as family counseling, and filing federal claims. The Veterans Healthcare Division operates eight Minnesota Veterans Homes located in Bemidji, Fergus Falls, Hastings, Luverne, Minneapolis, Montevideo, Preston, and Silver Bay. The three newest facilities in Bemidji, Montevideo, and Preston opened in early 2024. MDVA works to ensure Veterans and their families have options for meeting long-term care needs while providing the highest-quality care. The division offers multiple options for Veterans as well as their families including 24- hour skilled nursing, domiciliary, adult day care, and pharmaceutical needs. The division provides specialty care units for Alzheimer’s as well as dementia, oral health care initiative, physical rehabilitation, work therapy, transportation, chemical dependency, psychological, spiritual, and recreational services to improve the health along with quality of life of our Residents. In addition, the Healthcare Division leads a robust effort to reduce Veteran suicide rates in Minnesota and has most recently engaged in strategic planning and program development for a Veteran health navigator program to further connect Veterans with their state and federal benefits when seeking healthcare services in the community. Rationale/Background: The cost of operations rises each year due to increases in employer-paid health care contributions, FICA and Medicare, along with other salary and compensation-related costs. Other operating costs, like rent and lease, fuel and utilities, and IT and legal services also grow. This cost growth puts pressure on agency operating budgets that remain flat year to year. Agencies face challenging decisions to manage these rising costs within existing budgets, while maintaining the services Minnesotans expect. From year to year, agencies find ways to become more efficient with existing resources. For the Minnesota Department of Veterans Affairs, the following efficiencies have been implemented to help offset rising operating costs: • The Minnesota Department of Veterans Affairs has engaged in information technology solutions to enhance the stakeholder and client experience. These solutions generate an efficient, streamlined process for applicants to apply for earned benefits, receive timely status notifications, and decisions on their claim. The process improvement ensures program integrity, and the eligibility criteria is met per statute. • The Minnesota Department of Veterans Affairs has also prioritized hiring of staff at Minnesota Veterans homes and greatly reduced the use of costly supplemental contract staffing pools. Additionally, the Health Care Division has worked to reduce costs and optimize existing ancillary service contracts. For FY 2026-27, agencies will need to continue to find additional efficiencies and leverage management tools to help address budget pressures. Holding open vacancies in certain programs or delaying hiring in other programs are examples of ways agencies manage through constrained operating budgets. Such decisions are difficult and must be weighed against a program’s ability to conduct business with less staffing and its impact to service delivery. Agencies will need additional tools and flexibility, similar to those available in the private sector and other government entities, to help address operating pressures in upcoming biennium. Without additional resources to address these cost pressures, both in funding and in flexibility to manage internal budgets, services delivered to Minnesotans will be impacted.State of Minnesota 11 2026-27 Biennial Budget January 2025 Proposal: The Governor recommends increasing agency operating budgets to support the delivery of current services. For the Minnesota Department of Veterans Affairs, this funding will ensure the eight Minnesota State Veterans Homes in Minneapolis, Silver Bay, Fergus Falls, Luverne, Preston, Montevideo, Bemidji, and Hastings are able to continue operations. This funding will help cover expected growth in employee compensation and insurance, IT services and other operating costs. Furthermore, this operating adjustment will ensure that an additional $26 million in Federal VA Per Diems, Resident Maintenance Fees and Medicare Reimbursements will continue to be collected to support the operation of MN State Veterans Homes in the FY 26-27 biennium. The department is requesting funds to cover compensation costs for 185 FTE’s in FY 2026 and 210 FTE’s in FY 2027. Without this additional funding the department will need to lay-off staff in our Veteran Homes. The impact will be a reduction in service and not permit the hiring of the necessary staff for the new homes to reach the optimal census in the number of beds that are available to care for Veterans and their spouses. We anticipate this to result in a census occupancy of no more than 75% in all MN Veterans Homes, versus the current 90% that we are obligated to be at per federal Veterans Administration regulations. With federal expectations for census coupled with the current MN Veterans Home waiting lists average time of 9 months – 18 months depending on location, Veteran access to care will be negatively impacted causing significant stakeholder feedback and issues with regulatory compliance. Furthermore, the department is requesting additional funds to support non-salary operating funds to cover expected cost increases for medical supplies & equipment, utilities, resident food, laundry, housekeeping, pharmaceuticals, facility maintenance and other non-salary expenses. In addition, the expected increases in MNIT technology services due to increased compensation costs along with expected rate and usage cost increases are needed to support and maintain the Veterans Homes operations. Being a ‘Gold Star Family’ is a title no family wants. Initiated during World War I, it distinguished a family who lost a loved one in military service. Today, immediate family members (parent, sibling, spouse, or child) receive a Gold Star from the Department of Defense to honor those who made the ultimate sacrifice. The Blue Star Family tradition is rich in history and also dates to World War I. When an individual enters uniformed service and joins the military community, some military families choose to display a Blue Star service banner or flag in a window of their home. The ‘Blue Star’ signifies that a loved one is an active-duty service member. This special flag, typically displayed only by military families, is the genesis of “Blue Star Family”, referring to the immediate family of an active-duty service member. A Gold Star Families and Blue Star Families plaque would be appropriate within the context of the plaques already in the Court. Department of Veterans Affairs Program Narrative Program: Veterans Healthcare https://mn.gov/mdva/ AT A GLANCE • Operate eight State Veterans Homes in Bemidji, Fergus Falls, Hastings, Luverne, Minneapolis, Montevideo, Preston and Silver Bay • Provide 24/7 Skilled Nursing and Domiciliary Care • Achieved Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) Certification at four skilled nursing homes, with pending applications in our three newest State Veterans Homes • Achieved United States Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) recognition for three new Veterans Homes in 2024 • Operate one of only three Adult Day Healthcare Programs (Medical Model) in the country • Employ approximately 1,215 staff to care for Veterans • Focus on Veteran Suicide Prevention and Awareness • Launching Veteran Community Health Navigator program • Work closely with state, federal, and community partners to enhance all State Veterans Homes Resident’s lives PURPOSE AND CONTEXT The first Minnesota Veterans Home was established in 1887 in Minneapolis as the Minnesota Soldiers Home to “reward the brave and deserving.” This Soldiers Home in Minneapolis was not primarily designed as a skilled nursing facility, but as the needs of Veterans changed, so too have the Veterans Homes. In the 1960s, Minnesota started shifting its primary focus to meet the healthcare needs of Veteran Residents in the Federal Veterans Administration State Veterans Home program. Today, the Minnesota Department of Veteran’s Affairs (MDVA) operates eight Veterans Homes located in Bemidji, Fergus Falls, Hastings, Luverne, Minneapolis, Montevideo, Preston, and Silver Bay; and one Adult Day Care program located in Minneapolis. Healthcare Division initiatives are reviewed annually to ensure both quality care and operational excellence are achieved in all Veteran Homes. The initiatives detailed below were developed with staff as well as stakeholder input. The Veteran Homes were the primary focus in fiscal year 2023, including agency-level policy and procedures. Our goal is to always provide quality Resident care outcomes in state-of-the-art facilities with the highest level of efficiency. Many stakeholder groups are engaged in our Division performance, and we strive to align with both State and Federal goals impacting aging Veterans. The MDVA Healthcare Division’s goal is to ensure all healthcare facilities and programs are coordinating as one agency providing the highest level of care. In addition to the initiatives noted below, a primary focus of this past year was to stabilize the Healthcare Division, ensuring the growth of three new facilities is incorporated into the overall organizational structure. An additional focus area is to redesign an organizational chart that ensures continuity of services during transitions and succession planning. Calendar year 2023 included the following initiatives (not an inclusive list but highlights of top priorities): 1. COVID-19 Pandemic Emergency Response Transition Goal: Under the guidance of MDVA Healthcare senior leaders, each facility continues to refine an Infection Prevention (IP) strategy which includes the latest guidance from CMS, the Minnesota Department of Health (MDH), Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), and Department of Labor and Industry (DOLI). This allows all facilities to minimize the negative effects of all infectious diseases, including COVID-19, and followState of Minnesota 31 2026-27 Biennial Budget January 2025 an operational plan that monitors the impacts to a community, minimizes emergency response during outbreak, and continues to provide the highest quality of care. 2. Clinical Education/Best Practice – End of Life Care Education Program Goal: Enhance all areas of palliative care, advance care planning, and hospice in all Veterans Homes to provide Residents with dignity at end of life. Continue to offer as well as lead in clinical training programs to improve and enhance the quality of care delivered to Residents specifically during end of life along with prepare to meet the new healthcare needs of our current aging Veteran population. 3. Three New MN Veterans Homes Goal: Successfully open the three new Minnesota Veterans Homes and achieve federal VA recognition. All Homes have successfully opened, admitted their first Residents, and achieved Federal VA recognition. Homes will be at 75% occupancy by end of fiscal year 2025. Going forward, a primary focus will be efforts to continue to recruit and hire, in addition to admitting Residents along with ensuring that quality of care remains excellent. 4. Healthcare Operations Goal: Maximize revenues available through all possible means available to include federal VA per diem, Medicare, insurance, and Medicare Part D. Reviews of all Resident reimbursement options are ongoing. Primary areas of focus have been census realignment along with maximization, a comprehensive Healthcare Division recruitment as well as retention strategy, and an intentional collaboration of the admissions process, ensuring that all Veterans maximize their available benefits. In addition, a review of Minnesota Administrative Rule 9050 with recommended revisions will be completed this year. 5. Domiciliary Program Quality of Care Goal: MDVA facilitated a Quality-of-Care workgroup to evaluate Resident/Veteran care needs, staffing requirements, and program enhancements to further improve the care delivery model. In addition, State funding was received for the Hastings campus as well as design work is in progress as we wait for a decision from the Federal VA State Construction Grant Program. 6. Veteran Suicide Prevention and Awareness. Goal: MDVA continues working to reduce Veteran suicide rates in Minnesota. Current data shows approximately 100 Veterans die by suicide annually in Minnesota. MDVA leads the Minnesota Veteran Suicide Prevention and Awareness Collaborative (formerly called the “Governor’s Challenge to Reduce Veteran Suicide”). MDVA is actively partnering with the Minnesota Department of Health, the Federal VA, Minnesota Army National Guard, County Veteran Service Officers, Veterans Service Organizations, and other community partners on this effort. 7. Veteran Health Navigator Program Goal: MDVA has begun strategic planning and development of the Veteran Health Navigator program. The program targets active military and Veterans, connecting them to Veteran resources at all levels (community, State, and Federal), engaging them in preventive care as well as assisting with management in care coordination programs. All MDVA State Veterans Homes have the goal of restoring, optimizing, and/or maintaining each Resident’s level of function, personal autonomy, and dignity as well as recognizing the individual’s service to the country. The following programs and services are offered in each Veterans Home: • Specialty care units for those diagnosed with dementia • Rehabilitation services – including occupational, speech, physical therapy, and general physical fitness • Social services, recreational therapy and behavioral services • Coordination of specialty appointments with outside providers • Life enhancement activities – Entertainment, games and outdoor activities • Drug and alcohol supportive services • Vocational rehabilitation • On-site specialty services – dental, ophthalmology, audiology, podiatry • Primary Care and some Specialty Care In recent years, the Healthcare Division senior leadership team, with a strong commitment to Veteran-centric care, has focused primarily on the transition from the COVID-19 pandemic emergency response to the construction and opening of three new Minnesota Veterans Homes. Minnesota was the first state in the country to ever open three new Veterans Homes at one time. The accomplishment required the leadership team to provide frequent and onsite support to ensure successful openings along with meeting regulatory requirements. The project will develop approximately 1.2 acres. The construction will include 742 pre-placed crypts. The estimated total cost of the project is $1,368,000. The department already received approval of the use of these grant funds from the Legislative Advisory Commission in March of 2024. MDVA agrees that upon completion of the project, it will provide adequate financial support and a trained workforce to maintain and operate the cemetery in accordance with National Cemetery Administration standards. MDVA agrees that it will operate the cemetery solely for the interment of veterans, their spouses, surviving spouses, minor children, unmarried adult children who were physically or mentally disabled and incapable of self-support, and eligible parents of certain deceased service members. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) - Construction Grants for State Veterans Homes The MN Department of Veterans Affairs is currently requesting federal grant funds for a replacement bed project to update the Veterans Home in Hastings. The Federal VA’s construction grant program for State Veterans Homes requires the State to provide 35% of the funding for the predesign, design and construction of a new Home or remodel of an existing one. During the 2022 MN Legislative Session, state funds were allocated to the design, reconstruct, and update the Hastings Veterans Home. All requirements at this time have been met by the MDVA and the State of Minnesota to be considered for this project, however at this time the agency hasn’t received notice of approval of these federal funds to move forward with the project.
